Chapter 1: Arjuna Vishada Yoga

рдЕрд░реНрдЬреБрдирд╡рд┐рд╖рд╛рджрдпреЛрдЧ

The Yoga of Arjuna's Despondency - This chapter sets the stage for the entire Bhagavad Gita, describing Arjuna's moral crisis on the battlefield of Kurukshetra.

ЁЯУЦ Note on This Chapter

This chapter contains the setting and context for Krishna's teachings. Shankara Bhagavadpada did not provide a detailed verse-by-verse commentary (bhashya) for this chapter, as it serves primarily as the narrative introduction to the philosophical teachings that begin in Chapter 2.

Chapter Overview

The first chapter opens with King Dhritarashtra asking Sanjaya about the events on the battlefield of Kurukshetra. Sanjaya narrates how both armies, the Kauravas and Pandavas, assembled ready for battle.

When Arjuna surveys the battlefield and sees his teachers, relatives, and friends on both sides, he is overwhelmed with compassion and moral confusion. His arguments against fighting appear ethical and compassionate, but are rooted in attachment and ignorance of his true nature.

This despondency (vishada) becomes the catalyst for Krishna's teaching. Arjuna's crisis represents the universal human condition of confusion about duty, right action, and the nature of the Self.
