Advaita Vedanta

Advaita Vedanta, the non-dualistic school of Vedanta, teaches that Brahman (the absolute reality) and Atman (the individual self) are identical. This fundamental truth - that you are not the limited body-mind complex, but the infinite, eternal consciousness itself - is revealed through the study of the Upanishads under proper guidance.

Sri Adi Shankaracharya (788-820 CE) systematized this philosophy through his brilliant commentaries on the Prasthana Trayi and composed numerous prakarana granthas (introductory texts) to make these profound teachings accessible to qualified students.

"Brahman alone is real, the world is mithya (dependent reality), and the jiva (individual) is none other than Brahman."

- Core teaching of Advaita Vedanta

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Guidance

Study Under a Qualified Guru: The texts presented here are meant to be studied under the guidance of a qualified teacher (guru) who has realized the truth of Advaita Vedanta. Self-study without proper guidance can lead to misunderstanding and intellectual confusion.

The Guru-Shishya Parampara: Traditional Vedantic knowledge is transmitted through the unbroken lineage of teacher and student. A qualified guru can unfold the subtle meanings, remove doubts, and guide the student systematically toward realization.

Qualifications for Study: The student should possess the four-fold qualifications (sadhana chatushtaya): discrimination between eternal and non-eternal, dispassion, six virtues (mental control, sense control, withdrawal, forbearance, faith, concentration), and intense desire for liberation.

This platform serves as a reference resource and companion to traditional study, not as a replacement for the guru-shishya relationship.
