┼Ъaс╣Еkara Bhagavadp─Бda's commentary establishes that avidy─Б here specifically means karma (rituals like agnihotra), not metaphysical ignorance, while vidy─Б means up─Бsan─Б (deity-meditation) leading to deva-loka, not supreme Brahman-knowledge. The condemnation is not of these practices themselves - both are ┼Ы─Бstra-vihita (scripture-enjoined) with their own legitimate fruits (pitс╣Ы-loka for karma, deva-loka for vidy─Б per Bс╣Ы.Up. 1.5.16). Rather, what is condemned is exclusive practice (ekaik─Бnuс╣гс╣нh─Бna) of either one alone. The verse teaches that those who practice karma exclusively without vidy─Б enter blind darkness, while those who abandon karma and practice only vidy─Б enter even greater darkness. This establishes the foundation for the samuccaya teaching in subsequent mantras - that karma and vidy─Б should be combined appropriately. This teaching is specifically for householders who still have desires (k─Бmin), not for the desireless renunciates (ak─Бmin) established in Self-knowledge for whom such combination is impossible and unnecessary.

Exclusive devotion to either karma (rituals) alone or up─Бsan─Б (deity-meditation) alone leads to darkness. Neither should be practiced in isolation - they must be combined appropriately for those qualified householders who cannot yet take up the path of renunciation and Self-knowledge.
