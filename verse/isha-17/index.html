Notes

This mantra is the dying person's prayer for the soul's journey after death. ┼Ъaс╣Еkara Bhagavadp─Бda explains that v─Бyu (the individual pr─Бс╣Зa) should transcend its individual limitation and merge into the cosmic Anila (the S┼лtr─Бtman or cosmic thread connecting all beings). The subtle body (liс╣Еga-┼Ыar─лra), purified through knowledge and action, should depart on the upward path. The physical body is offered to fire to be reduced to ashes. The seeker then addresses the mind (kratu) as Agni, asking it to remember all the spiritual practices and karmas performed throughout life - this remembrance ensures the proper path after death. Om represents Brahman in the form of Agni/Truth. The repetition emphasizes the urgency and importance at this critical moment of transition.

рдореБрдЦреНрдп рдмреЛрдз (Central Teaching)

At the time of death, the qualified seeker prays: may the individual pr─Бс╣Зa merge into the cosmic S┼лtr─Бtman (immortal thread-soul), may the subtle body depart on the upward path, and may the physical body be cremated. The mind is invoked to remember all spiritual practices, ensuring the proper post-death journey to Brahman.
