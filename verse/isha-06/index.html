Notes

This mantra describes the vision of the realized sage (parivrя┐╜я┐╜с╣н). ┼Ъaс╣Еkara Bhagavadp─Бda emphasizes that this is not a prescription but a description (anuv─Бda) of what naturally follows from Self-knowledge. The key insight is that all aversion (ghс╣Ыс╣З─Б/vijugups─Б) requires duality - seeing something other than oneself as defiled. When only the pure, non-dual Self is seen everywhere, there is no second thing to cause aversion. The commentary stresses that the Self is the witness-consciousness (sarvapratyayas─Бkс╣гibh┼лta), the animating principle (cetayit─Б) that is alone and without qualities.

рдореБрдЦреНрдп рдмреЛрдз (Central Teaching)

The liberated sage sees all beings as non-different from the Self and sees the same Self in all beings. This vision of unity (ekatva-dar┼Ыana) naturally results in the absence of all aversion or disgust. Since aversion requires seeing something other than oneself as defiled, when only the pure Self is seen everywhere, there is no basis for any repulsion.
