Notes

This is one of the most profound mantras describing the paradoxical nature of Brahman. ┼Ъaс╣Еkara Bhagavadp─Бda resolves the apparent contradiction between being motionless and faster than mind by using the distinction between nirup─Бdhika (without limiting adjuncts) and sop─Бdhika (with limiting adjuncts). The Self is truly changeless, but through the mind as up─Бdhi, It illuminates all mental activities instantly. The commentary emphasizes that all cosmic and individual activities rest upon the Self as their unchanging substrate.

рдореБрдЦреНрдп рдмреЛрдз (Central Teaching)

The Self (─Аtman) is simultaneously motionless in Its essential nature yet appears swifter than mind through Its reflection in the mental faculty. This paradox is resolved by distinguishing the Self's unconditioned (nirup─Бdhika) nature from Its conditioned (sop─Бdhika) appearance. The Self is the unchanging substratum upon which all activities of the mind, senses, and cosmic forces depend.
