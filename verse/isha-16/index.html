Notes

This mantra continues and completes the prayer begun in verse 15. The seeker addresses the Sun deity with multiple epithets emphasizing His roles as nourisher, sole mover, controller, and offspring of Praj─Бpati. The prayer asks Him to withdraw His dazzling rays and radiance so that the supreme form behind can be seen. ┼Ъaс╣Еkara Bhagavadp─Бda emphasizes this is not a servant's request but a declaration of identity - the seeker recognizes the Puruс╣гa in the solar orb and declares 'I am He' (so'ham asmi). This is the culmination of Self-knowledge, the mah─Бv─Бkya declaring non-difference between the individual self and supreme Self. The commentary explains three derivations of 'puruс╣гa': having human form, filling all with consciousness, or dwelling in the city of the body.

рдореБрдЦреНрдп рдмреЛрдз (Central Teaching)

After requesting the Sun deity to withdraw His blinding rays, the qualified seeker prays to see the most auspicious supreme form. Upon seeing it, the declaration is made: 'That Person who is there - I am He' (so'ham asmi). This is the supreme non-dual realization of identity with Brahman.
