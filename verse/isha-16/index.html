Īśa Upaniṣad 16

पूषन्नेकर्षे यम सूर्य प्राजापत्य व्यूह रश्मीन्समूह तेजो यत्ते रूपं कल्याणतमं तत्ते पश्यामि योऽसावसौ पुरुषः सोऽहमस्मि ॥ १६ ॥

IAST Transliteration ⊕

pūṣann ekarṣe yama sūrya prājāpatya vyūha raśmīn samūha tejo yat te rūpaṃ kalyāṇatamaṃ tat te paśyāmi yo 'sāv asau puruṣaḥ so 'ham asmi || 16 ||

अनुवाद (Translation)

"O Pūṣan (Nourisher), sole traveler, O Yama (Controller), O Sūrya (Sun), offspring of Prajāpati - withdraw your rays, gather your radiance. That most auspicious form of yours, that I see. That Person who is there - I am He."

— Traditional

"O Pūṣan, the sole seer, O Controller, O Sun, offspring of Prajāpati, marshal your rays, withdraw your light. I shall see that form of yours which is most auspicious. I am indeed that Person."

— Swami Gambhirananda

श्री शङ्कर भगवद्पाद भाष्य

हे पूषन् जगतः पोषणात्पूषा रविः । तथा एक एव ऋषति गच्छतीत्येकर्षिः हे एकर्षे । तथा सर्वस्य संयमनाद्यमः हे यम । तथा रश्मीनां प्राणानां रसानां च स्वीकरणात्सूर्यः हे सूर्य । प्रजापतेरपत्यं प्राजापत्यः हे प्राजापत्य । व्यूह विगमय रश्मीन् स्वान् । समूह एकीकुरु उपसंहर तेजः तावकं ज्योतिः । यत् ते तव रूपं कल्याणतमम् अत्यन्तशोभनम् , तत् ते तवात्मनः प्रसादात् पश्यामि । किञ्च, अहं न तु त्वां भृत्यवद्याचे योऽसौ आदित्यमण्डलस्थः असौ व्याहृत्यवयवः पुरुषः पुरुषाकारत्वात् , पूर्णं वानेन प्राणबुद्ध्यात्मना जगत्समस्तमिति पुरुषः ; पुरि शयनाद्वा पुरुषः । सोऽहम् अस्मि भवामि ॥

Notes

This mantra continues and completes the prayer begun in verse 15. The seeker addresses the Sun deity with multiple epithets emphasizing His roles as nourisher, sole mover, controller, and offspring of Prajāpati. The prayer asks Him to withdraw His dazzling rays and radiance so that the supreme form behind can be seen. Śaṅkara Bhagavadpāda emphasizes this is not a servant's request but a declaration of identity - the seeker recognizes the Puruṣa in the solar orb and declares 'I am He' (so'ham asmi). This is the culmination of Self-knowledge, the mahāvākya declaring non-difference between the individual self and supreme Self. The commentary explains three derivations of 'puruṣa': having human form, filling all with consciousness, or dwelling in the city of the body.

मुख्य बोध (Central Teaching)

After requesting the Sun deity to withdraw His blinding rays, the qualified seeker prays to see the most auspicious supreme form. Upon seeing it, the declaration is made: 'That Person who is there - I am He' (so'ham asmi). This is the supreme non-dual realization of identity with Brahman.

तात्त्विक शब्द (Core Concepts)

सोऽहम् (so'ham)

so'ham

I am He; mahāvākya declaring identity between individual self and supreme Self/Brahman

एकर्षि (ekarṣi)

ekarṣi

Sole traveler/mover; one who alone moves through the sky, epithet of the Sun

कल्याणतम (kalyāṇatama)

kalyāṇatama

Most auspicious, supremely beautiful; the ultimate form of Brahman beyond the dazzling covering

आदित्यमण्डलस्थ (āditya-maṇḍala-stha)

āditya-maṇḍala-stha

Dwelling in the solar disc; the supreme Puruṣa residing in the Sun as its inner reality

पुरि शयन (puri śayana)

puri śayana

Dwelling in the city; etymological derivation - Puruṣa as one dwelling in the body (city)
